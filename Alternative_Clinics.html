Alternative Clinic Distance Risk

CRITICAL RISK

KD Terap → KK Kota Kuala Muda (43.9km)

This represents the longest routing distance in the registry. Road access is compromise-prone during Kuala Muda floods, risking complete isolation. Pre-stocking emergency packages is highly recommended.

HIGH RISK

KD Bukit Meriam → KK Bedong (20.1km)

Significant distance for emergency ambulance response during major flood events. Delayed transfers present clinical safety issues for acute evacuations from the Bukit Meriam sub-district.

COVERAGE GAP

Unprotected Facilities

Several critical facilities in the state registry currently lack any officially designated alternative clinics. No pre-planned contingency routing exists, causing acute vulnerability during evacuations.

Unprotected Facilities Registry

  • KKIA Jitra (Kubang Pasu) No Alternative
  • KK Kepala Batas (Kota Setar) No Alternative
  • KK Alor Mengkudu (Kota Setar) No Alternative
  • KD Tanjung Dawai (Kuala Muda) No Alternative
  • KD Kg Raja (Kuala Muda) No Alternative

Actionable Mitigation Measures

1. Assign Backup Sites: Formally designate fallback locations for all 5 unprotected clinics before the October monsoon period to establish operational continuity.
2. Pre-position Medical Stockpiles: Establish a dedicated 2-week supply of pharmaceuticals and PPE at Kuala Muda alternative clinics to buffer the 43.9km transit gap if roads are cut.
3. Early Staff Pre-deployment: Deploy clinical and health teams to designated alternatives *before* flood waters sever local roads to ensure service remains uninterrupted.
